Fermions

Fermions compose all matter. For example a hydrogen (1H, protium, not an ion) atom would be composed of 1 Proton, 2 Up Quarks and 1 Down Quark, and 1 Electron. There are two types of of Fermions: Quarks and Leptons. Quarks make up Nucleons (Protons, Neutrons, others described later). Leptons consist of Electrons and Neutrinos. Electrons create the bases of Chemistry and are harnessed for modern electricity. Neutrinos are very low in mass and neutral in charge, making them very hard to detect (they are still being studied).

Bosons

Bosons are force carriers, they "tell" Fermions how to interact with other Fermions. For Example in a Proton, Gluons make up a Gluon Field that holds Quarks together.
